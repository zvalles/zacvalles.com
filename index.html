Zac Valles

I studied aerospace engineering at UMichigan and spent some time there researching fluid mechanics to build supercavitating hydrofoils and torpedos. Before that, I studied mechanical engineering at UCR where a few friends and myself developed one of the first AWD electric vehicle's in the North American Formula SAE competition.

Currently I work at Tesla on the powertrain engineering team. I work on driving innovation into castings and vibration isolation systems of the powertrain and chassis, playing a role in every vehicle Tesla develops. I love to build. From consumer products that emphasize design vision and human interaction to solving multidisplinary physics problems in heat transfer, structural mechanics, manufacturing engineering and material science, I love to build.

I made this website to document my career, personal projects, and writings about life which you'll find below.
